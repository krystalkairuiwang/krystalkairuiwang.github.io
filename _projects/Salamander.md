Fall 2024

In EGR101, we were tasked with devising a method of salamander population sampling that is minimally invasive and operatable by one researcher. The main issue the researchers faced was needing to lift up coverboards without trampling on the surrounding forrest floor.

<img src="/assets/images/coverboard.jpg" alt="coverboard" style="width:500px;">
Researchers lift these coverboards to count salamanders underneath it.

Throughout the design process, we interviewed and connected with our client to understand their prioritized design criteria and receive feedback on the design effectiveness. We began the project by brainstorming and combining different solutions, using pugh screening matrix to evaluate rank the designs. As we went from low-fidelity to high-fidelity prototypes, I played a role in integrating the different parts that we worked on separately, making sketches and technical drawings and assembling the pieces together in the workshop.

<img src="/assets/images/salamander_desgin.jpg" alt="design" style="width:500px;">
The sketch of the final design that I brought to the team meeting.

As a part of the course, we learned to write technical memos reporting our research, progress, and justification of decisions. We also had the opportunity to present our project both as a presentation and as a poster.
